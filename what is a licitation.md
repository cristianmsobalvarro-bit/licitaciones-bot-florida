# What is a Licitation (Bid)? 

A **licitation** (commonly referred to as a **bid** or **tender** in the United States) is a formal procurement process used by government entities, public institutions, and large private organizations to solicit proposals for goods, services, or construction projects. 

When an entity (like a city, county, or school district) needs a job done, they publish a detailed project specification and invite contractors to submit competitive offers. The contract is usually awarded to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder.

## What is a Roofing Bid?

A **roofing bid** specifically targets projects related to the installation, repair, replacement, or maintenance of roof systems on public or commercial buildings. These projects can range from minor patch repairs on a local library to multimillion-dollar complete roof replacements on massive school campuses or government complexes.

Roofing bids typically require:
- Detailed cost estimates (labor, materials, overhead).
- Proof of insurance and specific contractor licenses.
- Bonding capacity (Bid Bonds, Performance Bonds).
- Strict adherence to project specifications and safety codes.

## How are Bids Found?

Finding government bids is historically a fragmented and tedious process. Government entities publish their solicitations on various platforms:
- **State Portals:** e.g., MyFloridaMarketPlace.
- **Third-Party SaaS Platforms:** Many cities and counties outsource their procurement portals to software companies like DemandStar, Bonfire, OpenGov, BidNet, or VendorLink.
- **Custom/Legacy Websites:** Some local governments maintain their own custom-built, often outdated, procurement websites.

## How Do You Participate?

Participating in a bid generally involves the following steps:
1. **Registration:** You must register as a vendor on the specific portal the agency uses.
2. **Reviewing Documents:** Downloading and carefully reading the project specifications, blueprints, and requirements.
3. **Pre-Bid Meeting:** Attending mandatory or optional site visits to inspect the roof and ask questions.
4. **Estimation & Proposal:** Calculating costs and assembling the formal bid package.
5. **Submission:** Submitting the sealed bid electronically or physically before the strict deadline.

## Why is This Bot Necessary?

### The Fragmentation Problem
In the state of Florida alone, there are 67 counties, hundreds of cities, and numerous special districts (schools, water management, ports). These entities **do not share a centralized database**. Instead, their bids are scattered across hundreds of different digital portals.

### The Inefficiency of Manual Search
For a niche contractor, such as a roofing company, manually checking dozens or hundreds of different websites every single day to find new opportunities is virtually impossible and highly unfruitful. It requires immense administrative overhead, and by the time an opportunity is found manually, the deadline might be too close to prepare a competitive proposal.

### The Bot's Solution
This autonomous Bidding Bot solves the fragmentation problem by acting as an aggregator. It programmatically visits over 60 different portals, navigates through complex interfaces, bypasses anti-bot protections, and pulls all relevant roofing opportunities into a single, centralized list. This allows the contractor to focus on estimating and winning jobs, rather than endlessly hunting for them.
